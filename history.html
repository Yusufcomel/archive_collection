History

Kain Songket

Preserving Heritage, Stitch by Stitch

Kain Songket is a luxurious handwoven fabric traditionally made with silk or cotton and intricately patterned with gold or silver threads. Its origins can be traced back to the Malay courts of the 15th century, especially in the state of Kelantan and Terengganu. Often worn during royal ceremonies and weddings, songket symbolizes elegance, heritage, and social status. The weaving process requires exceptional skill and patience, making each piece unique and deeply meaningful.

Baju Kurung

The Baju Kurung is a classic traditional garment of Malaysia. This refined and sophisticated ensemble comprises a long-sleeved shirt and a coordinating skirt, typically donned by ladies of all ages, particularly during formal gatherings and celebratory occasions. It gained prominence under the Malacca Sultanate and diversified into several styles throughout Malaysia, such as the Baju Kurung Teluk Belanga (Johor) and Baju Kurung Kedah (northern Malaysia). It embodies cultural elegance and Malay femininity.

Baju Kebaya

Baju Kebaya is a traditional garment characterized by its form-fitting blouse accompanied by a batik or sarong. It derives from the Majapahit empire and was popularized by ladies in the Malay Archipelago, especially in Melaka, Indonesia, and Singapore. The kebaya, adorned with intricate embroidery and lacework, epitomizes femininity, sophistication, and cosmopolitan grace. Currently, it is prominently donned during cultural events and is frequently linked to the identity of contemporary Nyonya and Malay women.
